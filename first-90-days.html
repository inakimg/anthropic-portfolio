If I Had the Territory Tomorrow

A plan, not a promise. Most candidates tell you what they've done. Here's exactly what I'd do in the first 90 days - and what I'd be watching.

"The job isn't to sell AI. It's to be the trusted guide who helps a customer cross a once-in-a-generation shift - and to grow fastest what's already working."

Days 1-30 · Listen & Map

Understand the product, the team, and where the gold already is

  • Be a power user on day one. I can't guide a customer through a tool I don't live in. I already build with these models daily - I'll go deeper.
  • Meet the team, top customers, and partners. Learn the existing motion before changing anything.
  • Map the territory two ways: (1) the top logos in priority verticals, and (2) the accounts already using the product self-serve with no account team - the gold sitting in the data.
  • Score every account on expansion potential and AI-readiness. I already built the tool for this - 489 accounts mapped.
Days 31-60 · Land & Expand

Fast wins first: grow the users who already love it

  • Start where it's warmest: existing self-serve users with no account team. They already chose the product - they just never had anyone to help them scale it. Fastest path to revenue.
  • For each: align with the exec sponsor, map current usage, find expansion (seats, new use-cases, production workloads), and remove friction - procurement, security review, compliance.
  • In parallel, open the top target logos in priority verticals with technical POCs. Lead with the engineers, not the slides.
  • Lock in 2-3 reference stories early - momentum compounds.
Days 61-90 · Systematize & Scale

Turn wins into a repeatable machine

  • Codify what worked into a repeatable playbook: discovery → POC → expand, with the qualification gates that actually predict wins.
  • Activate partner & ecosystem leverage (cloud marketplaces, SIs) - marketplace deals mean near-zero procurement friction.
  • Define the coverage model and where the first SE / BDR support should sit.
  • Build a quarter of qualified pipeline with healthy coverage, and a named target list for the next two quarters.

What I'd Be Watching

  • Support coverage. Users with no account team often have no SE either. Growing them fast needs a scalable support motion, not just a closer.
  • Procurement & compliance friction in regulated verticals (FSI, healthcare, public sector) - I'd pre-map data-residency and security requirements before the first meeting.
  • Displacement timing. Many buyers are multi-model. Knowing when a contract or a pain point opens the window is half the battle.
  • Retention, not just land. The close is the beginning - usage and renewal are the real scoreboard.
  • Partner leverage. Marketplaces and SIs are force multipliers in APAC - one AE can't cover the region alone.
