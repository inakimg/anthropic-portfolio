How I Displaced OpenAI With a Multi-Model Router

A technical teardown

A customer running an agentic analytics platform - 2M+ users - was locked into a single OpenAI dependency. Fragmented LLM APIs, runaway cost, and security gaps were quietly blocking their entire enterprise pipeline. The incumbent answer was "use a bigger model." That's expensive and slow.

The real insight: not every request needs the most powerful model. Most production traffic is high-volume and structured; only a slice needs deep agentic reasoning. So instead of one model for everything, I co-architected a RAG complexity router with the customer's engineers.

The architecture: classify each incoming request by complexity, then route - Claude Opus for agentic reasoning and multi-step tasks, Gemini Flash for high-volume structured queries - all at sub-200ms latency. Best model for each job, not one model taxed for all of them.

The outcome: a $1M+/year contract won over OpenAI, 2x lower cost, a 15% lift in free-to-paid conversion, and an enterprise pipeline that was finally unblocked.

The lesson I keep relearning: in enterprise AI, technical credibility is the deal. The sellers who understand the architecture - who can sit with the engineers and design the routing logic - are the ones who win. That's the seller I've built myself into.

The Moment I Understood

February 2025

I've been selling AI for years. I thought I understood it. Then I started building with AI coding agents — and everything changed.

It started small. My wife and I needed a way to track family expenses. I'm not a developer. Never wrote production code in my life. But I thought — let me just try describing what I wanted to an AI agent.

Two hours later, I had a working expense tracker. Categories, monthly summaries, a clean interface. It wasn't perfect, but it worked. We still use it.

That was the first "aha" moment. The second came when I decided to build this portfolio.

9,700+ lines of code in a few days. Interactive maps, sales tools, strategy documents — all by describing what I wanted and iterating fast. Not because I learned to code. Because the AI handled the code while I focused on what I actually know: the business problem.

Andrej Karpathy talked about this — how AI coding agents feel like having a team of engineers. That's real. But what hit me deeper was something else.

This is the future. Not just for developers — for everyone who has ideas but lacks the technical skills to ship them. And that future needs to be built responsibly.

I help customers deploy AI across Southeast Asia. I've seen what happens when AI goes right — and when it goes wrong. Bad implementations, hallucinating systems in production, teams that didn't think through the edge cases.

That's why responsible AI isn't theoretical to me. It's what I work on every day. It's why I volunteer at AI Singapore - helping NGOs and the next generation adopt AI the right way.

The models that win long-term won't just be the most capable - they'll be the ones people trust. When I recommend a tool to a customer, I'm not just selling features. I'm putting my name on something I believe in.

Now I want to do this at the frontier - full-time.

There's always a way. You just need to find it.
