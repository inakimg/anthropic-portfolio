The Moment I Understood

February 2025

I've been selling AI for years. I thought I understood it. Then I started building with Claude Code — and everything changed.

It started small. My wife and I needed a way to track family expenses. I'm not a developer. Never wrote production code in my life. But I thought — let me just try describing what I want to Claude.

Two hours later, I had a working expense tracker. Categories, monthly summaries, a clean interface. It wasn't perfect, but it worked. We still use it.

That was the first "aha" moment. The second came when I decided to build this portfolio.

6,700+ lines of code in a few days. Interactive maps, sales tools, strategy documents — all by describing what I wanted and iterating fast. Not because I learned to code. Because Claude handled the code while I focused on what I actually know: the business problem.

Andrej Karpathy talked about this — how Claude Code feels like having a team of engineers. That's real. But what hit me deeper was something else.

This is the future. Not just for developers — for everyone who has ideas but lacks the technical skills to ship them. And that future needs to be built responsibly.

I help customers deploy AI across Southeast Asia. I've seen what happens when AI goes right — and when it goes wrong. Bad implementations, hallucinating systems in production, teams that didn't think through the edge cases.

That's why AI safety isn't theoretical to me. It's what I work on every day. It's why I volunteer at AI Singapore. And it's why I want to join Anthropic.

Claude isn't just capable — it's built with values. Constitutional AI, transparency, genuine helpfulness. When I recommend Claude to customers, I'm not just selling features. I'm recommending something I trust.

I want to do that full-time.

There's always a way. You just need to find it.
