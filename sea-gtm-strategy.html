SEA Go-To-Market Strategy

How I'd build Anthropic's Digital Natives business in Southeast Asia from the ground up.

The Opportunity

Southeast Asia has 700M+ people, 400M+ internet users, and the fastest-growing startup ecosystem outside the US and China. But it's not one market — it's six distinct markets that need a localized approach.

The insight: Digital natives in SEA are already using AI. They're not waiting for perfect solutions — they're shipping with whatever works. The question isn't whether they'll adopt AI, but whose AI they'll adopt.

GTM Approach

Build from the ground up. Start narrow, prove value, then expand. The playbook that worked for Google Cloud in Iberia and SEA.

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

Land 5-10 lighthouse accounts
  • Focus on Singapore-based AI-natives with regional ambitions
  • Target companies already using OpenAI/Azure OpenAI who have hit limitations
  • Offer deep technical engagement, not just sales
  • Document wins and build case studies

Phase 2: Partner Ecosystem (Months 3-6)

Build the channel
  • Identify 3-5 key system integrators with AI practices
  • Train partner SEs on Claude differentiation
  • Co-develop vertical solutions (fintech risk, healthtech triage)
  • Establish VC relationships (Antler, Sequoia SEA, Vertex)

Phase 3: Market Expansion (Months 6-12)

Scale what works
  • Expand to Indonesia and Vietnam with localized approach
  • Hire local AEs with startup ecosystem credibility
  • Launch developer community programs
  • Host Claude workshops at major tech events

Key principle: In SEA, relationships matter more than features. Being present at events, responsive on WhatsApp, and genuinely helpful builds trust faster than any marketing campaign.

Competitive Positioning

Anthropic isn't trying to be everything to everyone. The positioning should reflect what makes Claude genuinely different.

Against OpenAI

Lead with reasoning quality on complex tasks. Enterprise customers who've hit GPT-4's limits on nuanced analysis — financial modeling, legal review, technical documentation — are the ideal entry point. Don't compete on price; compete on output quality.

Against Google/Azure

Many SEA startups are already on GCP or Azure for infrastructure. Position Claude as complementary, not competitive. "Best model for the task" architecture. Claude for reasoning, Gemini Flash for volume, GPT-4o for specific use cases.

The Safety Angle

In SEA, "AI safety" isn't yet a buying criterion for most startups. But it matters for: (1) companies expanding to US/EU markets, (2) regulated industries like fintech and health, (3) government contracts. Position safety as enterprise-readiness, not philosophy.
