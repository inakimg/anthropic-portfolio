My great-grandfather started Moreno Antolinos in 1951. Fourth generation. First one lucky enough to study — paid by the hard work of everyone before me. One side of the family migrated from Murcia to Barcelona for a better life. The other side worked for wealthy families — my grandmother Angeles as a cook, my grandfather as a gardener. She saved on everything. Lights off. Same cooking oil for months. But every peso went to her grandchildren. Effort is unnegotiable. No task is too small. Treat everyone the same. My grandfather taught me at the butcher counter: give customers what they need, not what's expensive. They come back. Then Blanca was born. AI safety stopped being theoretical.

Three generations. Left: me with Blanca, 2024. Right: my grandmother Angeles with me, 1992. She always wore her bata — the worker's dress. Even on weekends. She never took it off.

Barcelona, ~1998. With my grandparents and brother. Every Christmas, the families she cooked for would come back with an envelope to thank her. That's how I learned: do good work, people remember.

Moreno Antolinos. Three generations at the family butcher shop. I learned the business working the counter — making sausages, serving customers, getting feedback. The full loop.

Singapore, 2018. 2nd Division. Captain since I was 10. 25+ years of football taught me: show up every day, no one is bigger than the team. I learned from teammates who walked 15km to train. Some couldn't afford new shoes. Some turned pro.

Singapore, 2024. Blanca, 13 months. This is why AI safety matters. Her generation will live with what we build today.

"There's always a way. You just need to find it."
